Thirds and Fifths
	- Major Third (4 semitones)
	- Minor Third (3 semitones) 
	- Perfect Fifth (7 semitone) 
	- Diminished Fifth (6 semitone)
	- Augmented Fifth (8 semitones
Chords
	- Major (Uppercase roman numeral)
		- Major Third, Perfect Fifth
	- Minor (Lowercase roman numeral)
		- Minor Third, Perfect Fifth
	- Diminished (Dim or a circle)
		- Minor Third, Diminished Fifth (2 minor third)
	- Augmented 
		- Major Third, Augmented Fifth  (2 major third)

Seventh (Most important)
	- Major 7            : 1 3  5  7    (Major chord + 7)
	- Dominant 7         : 1 3  5  b7   (Major chord + b7)
	- Minor 7            : 1 b3 5  b7   (Minor chord + b7)
	- In diatonic major scale
		- Major chords I , IV are diatonic major 7
		- Major chords V is the diatonic Dominant 7 which pulls strongly to tonal centre
		- Minor chords ii ii vi are all diatonic minor 7
	- Apply the same concept to the relative minor
		- Major - 6 7 1 2 3 4 5 
		- Minor - 1 2 3 4 5 6 7
			- Major    7 - III and VI
			- Dominant 7 - VII
			- Minor 7    - i iv v
