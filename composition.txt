Diatonic Chord Progression
	- Cadence
		- Perfect    : V - I
		- Plagal     : IV - 1
		- Imperfect  : I / II / IV / VI - V 
		- Interrupted: V - VI
	- Major (skip 7)
		- I - x - x - IV/V 
	- Minor 
		- use 1 7 6 
			- i - VII - VI 
			- VI - i - VII - i 
		- use 1 7 6 + 3 (careful to make i tonal centre)
			- i - VI - III - VII (SFCP)
		- use the v or V7 (switch to harmonic)
			- i - VI - iv - V7
			- i - VII - VI - V7 (andalusian cadence)
			- i - III - iv - VI -> i - III - VI - V7 
		- use the ii dim or ii m7b5 
			- ii - V7 - i 

Borrowed Chord (take chords from relative key or mode, with same tonal centre)
	- Minor key
		- Picardy Third: On the tonic chord, raise the third to change from minor chord to major 
	- Major key
		- bVI is common because it is from the aeolian scale 
		- bVII is from mixodylian scale 
		- I - bVII - bVI - bVII
		- I - bVI - IV
		- bVII - I instead of V - I (to a extent bII bIII bVI)
